John Davis II & John Davis III

Concord, Massachusetts to Reading, Vermont  ·  1715–1808
Dolor→ Simon I→ Dr. Simon→ Dr. John→ John Davis II (b. 1715)→ John Davis III (b. 1735)→ Jonathan→ ...→ William Elbridge

Two generations of John Davis carry the line from colonial Massachusetts to the Vermont frontier. John Davis II was born in Concord the year George I became king of England; his son John Davis III was a Vermont pioneer who built the first sawmill above Hammondsville and came with ten children to Reading, Vermont — crossing the mountains just as the new American republic was being born.

John Davis II
born July 15, 1715, Concord, Massachusetts  ·  died October 8, 1753, Littleton, Massachusetts
WE Line · Generation VJohn Davis II is the eldest son of Dr. John Davis and Abigail Dudley, and great-great-great-grandson of Dolor Davis. He married Hannah Johnson and had seven children before dying at 38 in Littleton, Massachusetts.
July 15, 1715
Birth in Concord, Massachusetts
John II was born on July 15, 1715 in Concord — the same town where his father and grandfather had practiced medicine and built their lives. He was the eldest of Dr. John Davis's seven children.
c. 1734
Marriage to Hannah Johnson
John II married Hannah Johnson. The exact date and Hannah's origins are not recorded in the available sources. Together they had seven children, the eldest of whom — John III — was born in 1735.
Wife
Hannah Johnson Davis
dates unknown · 7 children
Oct. 8, 1753
Death at Littleton, Massachusetts, aged 38
John II died young on October 8, 1753, in Littleton, Massachusetts — leaving Hannah with seven children, the youngest just three years old. His son John III, then 18, would carry the line forward and eventually lead his own family to Vermont.

John Davis III · Generation VI — Vermont Pioneer
J3
John Davis III
born June 1, 1735, Groton, Massachusetts  ·  died May 1808, Reading, Vermont
WE Line · Generation VIJohn Davis III is the eldest son of John II and Hannah Johnson. He married Hulda Thayer, came to Reading, Vermont in 1788 with ten children, and built the first sawmill above Hammondsville — one of the earliest settlers of that Vermont hill town.
June 2, 1757
Marriage to Hulda Thayer
John III married Hulda Thayer on June 2, 1757 — daughter of Benjamin and Mary Wilkinson Thayer. Hulda was born November 5, 1730. Together they had ten children over more than twenty years.
Wife
Hulda Thayer Davis
b. November 5, 1730 · daughter of Benjamin and Mary Wilkinson Thayer · 10 children · date of death uncertain
1788
Removal to Reading, Vermont
In 1788 — five years after the end of the Revolutionary War — John Davis III led his family north into the Vermont hills, settling in Reading, Vermont. He built the first sawmill just above Hammondsville, a hamlet in Reading, and became one of the town's pioneer settlers. John and Hulda are buried in Bailey's Mills Cemetery in Reading. In 1964, no grave markers for them were found.
Historical Context · Vermont 1788
Vermont had only been an independent republic since 1777, and would not join the United States until 1791. The hill towns of Windsor County were being settled rapidly after the Revolution, with families from Massachusetts and Connecticut pushing north and east. John Davis III was among the earliest wave of settlers in this region.
May 1808
Death at Reading, Vermont
John Davis III died in May 1808, in Reading, Vermont. He had lived to see Vermont become a state, the Constitution ratified, and two American presidents come and go. His son Jonathan carried the Davis line forward in West Windsor, Vermont.
