Generation VII

Jonathan Davis

West Windsor, Vermont  ·  1767–1856
Dolor→Simon I→ ...→John II→ John III→ Jonathan Davis→ Jonas→Oscar→William Elbridge

Jonathan Davis came to Vermont with his family as a young man, bought land in West Windsor in 1794, and spent more than sixty years there as farmer, cordwainer, and the founding moderator of the first school in the region. He is the Davis who settled the family in Windsor County for generations to come.

1767/68
Birth in Shirley, Massachusetts
Jonathan Davis was born in 1767 or 1768 in Shirley, Massachusetts — a discrepancy in the records, with page 11 of the family history giving 1767 and page 12 giving 1768. He was the fifth of John III and Hulda Thayer's ten children. He was a boy of 7 or 8 when the Revolution began at Lexington and Concord, and came of age entirely in the new republic.
1794
Arrival in West Windsor, Vermont
Jonathan came to Vermont with his family and bought land in West Windsor, Vermont in 1794 — just six years after his father John III had pioneered in nearby Reading. The property remained in the Davis family until 1845, when he sold it to his youngest brother Levi. Jonathan worked as both a farmer and a cordwainer — a shoemaker specializing in new leather, a skilled trade in rural New England.
circa1791
Marriage to Sarah Francis
Jonathan married Sarah Francis of Lunenburg, Massachusetts — daughter of Richard and Hannah Winship Francis. Sarah died August 19, 1849. Together they had eight children born in West Windsor over more than twenty years.

early1800s
Founding Moderator of the Broomshire School
A notebook found in the West Windsor town clerk's office contained the minutes of meetings regarding the first school in the region — called "Broomshire" in West Windsor. Jonathan Davis was the first moderator, and his name appears often in those records until about 1848 — a civic role spanning nearly half a century, the longest-serving civic presence of any Davis in the direct line. Source: American Ancestors and Descendants of William Elbridge Davis (2003), p. 12.
Apr. 11,
1856
Death at West Windsor, aged ~88
Jonathan Davis died on April 11, 1856 in West Windsor, Vermont — at approximately 88 years of age, one of the longest-lived members of the direct line. He had lived from the eve of the Revolution through the Mexican-American War. His son Jonas had already established the next generation. Source: American Ancestors and Descendants of William Elbridge Davis (2003), p. 11.
