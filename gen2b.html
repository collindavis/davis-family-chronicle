Concord, Massachusetts  ·  1636–1762  ·  The Branch from Dolor
Dolor Davis→ Lt. Simon Davis I→ Dr. Simon Davis→ Dr. John Davis→ John Davis II→ ...→ William Elbridge Davis

This branch begins where the Horace Davis line and the William Elbridge Davis line diverge — at Dolor's two sons. The Horace line descends through Samuel; this line descends through Lt. Simon I. Both are Dolor's grandchildren of the same generation, but the paths they took led one family to a Governor's mansion and the other to a Vermont farm — and, eventually, to you.

The Branch Point · Generation II
Two Sons of Dolor Davis
Dolor Davis and Margery Willard had two sons who survived to adulthood and founded lines: Simon I (b. c. 1636) and Samuel (b. unknown). Samuel's line leads to Horace Davis — the Governor, Senator, and author of the 1881 chronicle. Simon I's line leads to William Elbridge Davis of Hartland, Vermont — and through him, to the family documented here. The two lines run in parallel from 1636 to the present day, both tracing back to a carpenter from the Weald of Kent.
Lt. Simon Davis I · Generation II
SI
Simon Davis I, Lieutenant
born c. 1636, America  ·  died June 14, 1718, Concord, Massachusetts
WE Line · Generation II · Branch RootSimon I is the second child of Dolor Davis and Margery Willard — born in America, settled in Concord, and the patriarch of the William Elbridge Davis line. His brother Samuel's descendants became the Horace Davis line.
c. 1636
Born in America
Simon I was born in America — likely in Duxbury or Barnstable — during his father Dolor's years in Plymouth Colony. He was the second surviving son, growing up as the family moved repeatedly before settling in Concord, Massachusetts.
Dec. 12, 1660
Marriage to Mary Blood
Simon I married Mary Blood, daughter of James Blood of Concord, born July 12, 1640. They settled in Concord, where Simon became one of the town's most prominent figures.
Wife
Mary Blood Davis
b. July 12, 1640, Concord, Massachusetts · daughter of James Blood of Concord
Seven children mentioned in Simon's will as still living at the time of his death.
1675
King Philip's War — Command at Brookfield
Simon I commanded Massachusetts troops after Captain Wheeler was wounded at Brookfield in 1675 — one of the most brutal engagements of King Philip's War. His military service earned him the rank of Lieutenant, which attached to his name in all subsequent records.
1689–1705
Deputy to the General Court
Simon I served as Deputy to the Massachusetts General Court in 1689, 1690, 1692, and 1705 — one of the most significant civic roles available in colonial Massachusetts. His original will survives at East Cambridge, made June 24, 1710, in his own hand.
June 14, 1718
Death at Concord, aged 77
Simon I died June 14, 1718 at approximately 77 years of age. His gravestone still stands in the Concord burying-ground. Like his father Dolor, he had divided most of his real estate among his sons before death. His wife Mary and seven children survived him.

Dr. Simon Davis · Generation III
DS
Dr. Simon Davis
born October 12, 1661, Concord  ·  died after 1714, Concord, Massachusetts
Feb. 14, 1689
Physician of Concord · Marriage to Elizabeth Woodhouse
Dr. Simon Davis was one of the most distinguished physicians of his day in Concord, Massachusetts. He married Elizabeth Woodhouse, daughter of Henry Woodhouse of Concord, on February 14, 1689. Elizabeth died November 12, 1711; Simon remarried Mary Wood on October 19, 1714. He had eight children by his first wife.

Dr. John Davis · Generation IV

Dr. John Davis
born November 19, 1689, Concord, Massachusetts  ·  died November 16, 1762, Acton, Massachusetts
Dec. 1713
Physician at Concord · Marriage to Abigail Dudley
Dr. John Davis was a physician, born in Concord on November 19, 1689, and died in Acton on November 16, 1762, at age 72. His will was dated September 3, 1762. He married Abigail Dudley in December 1713 — born June 11, 1672, daughter of Joseph and Abigail Dudley.
