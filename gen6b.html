Generation XI · Your Great-GrandfatherElbridge Nathaniel — nicknamed "Ee" — is the ninth of twelve children of William Elbridge and Addie Barker Davis. He married Eudora Cleora Sykes in 1921 and raised five children in Windsor, Vermont. His son Arthur Edward Davis is your grandfather.
Mar. 13,
1891
Birth in Hartland, Vermont
Elbridge Nathaniel Davis was born on March 13, 1891 in Hartland, Vermont — the ninth of twelve children on the Davis farm at Hartland Four Corners. He grew up alongside his brothers and sisters attending the local schools and helping with farm work. His older brothers Harry and Ted would go on to own and operate the Davis Brothers Garage on Main Street Windsor — a Chevrolet and Oldsmobile dealership with service and body shop — and to breed and sell Morgan horses, prominent in regional horse shows. Elbridge came of age in this enterprising Vermont family. Source: American Ancestors and Descendants of William Elbridge Davis (2003), p. 118.
May 6,
1921
Marriage to Eudora Cleora Sykes
Elbridge married Eudora Cleora Sykes in Hartland, Vermont on May 6, 1921. The story of how they met is one worth telling: Elbridge served as best man and Eudora as maid of honor at the wedding of her cousin Beatrice Cushman to Millard Dana. Standing on opposite sides of that altar, they found their way to each other.

Daughter of Wilbert and Harriet Silver Sykes of West Windsor. Eudora outlived Elbridge by more than twenty years, dying in Burlington in 1976 at age 82.

1922
–1934
Five Children in Windsor, Vermont
Elbridge and Eudora raised five children in Windsor, Vermont over twelve years — Arthur Edward, Barbara Elaine, David A., Virginia A., and Harriet Ruth. The family was rooted in the Windsor County community that the Davis family had called home since Jonathan Davis arrived in West Windsor in 1794. Their children became notably accomplished: Barbara attended Smith College; David earned a law degree from the University of Florida; Virginia married into the Cochran family of Richmond, Vermont — Vermont's most famous skiing dynasty, whose children competed in the 1972 Sapporo Olympics and whose daughter Marilyn became the first American to win the French National Alpine Championship. Source: American Ancestors and Descendants of William Elbridge Davis (2003), pp. 118, 130, 137, 144.
July 19,
1954
Death at Windsor, Vermont, aged 63
Elbridge Nathaniel Davis died on July 19, 1954, in Windsor, Vermont, at age 63. His son Arthur was 32 years old at the time; his grandson Richard Prescott Davis — your father — was just four years old, born only in April 1950. Eudora survived him by more than two decades, dying in Burlington in January 1976. Source: American Ancestors and Descendants of William Elbridge Davis (2003), p. 118.

Notable · Virginia Davis & the Cochran Ski Family
Vermont's Most Famous Skiing Family
Elbridge's daughter Virginia Davis married Gordon "Mickey" Cochran of Richmond, Vermont in 1949. Their four children — Marilyn, Barbara, Robert, and Linda — became members of the National Alpine Ski Team and Vermont's most celebrated athletic family. Marilyn Cochran became the first American to win the French National Alpine Championship, and all four Cochran children were candidates for the 1972 Winter Olympics in Sapporo, Japan. The Cochran family ski area in Richmond, Vermont still bears the family name today — making Elbridge Nathaniel Davis the grandfather of one of the greatest skiing families in American history.
Primary source: American Ancestors and Descendants of William Elbridge Davis (2003)
